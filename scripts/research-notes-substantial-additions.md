## #5 Concrete data block (text-critical, manuscript-level)

### Matthew 6:13 doxology: attestation profile
- Longer doxology present in many later manuscripts (often Byzantine stream), with minor wording variation.
- Omission is supported by early/strong witnesses frequently listed in critical notes: ◊ê (Sinaiticus), B (Vaticanus), D (Bezae), Z, 0170, family 1, some Old Latin/Coptic witnesses, and Origen citations.
- Didache 8 preserves a shorter liturgical ending: effectively "for yours is the power and the glory forever" (without the full later kingdom-power-glory-amen form).

### Why this matters for chapter 3 specifically
- It demonstrates liturgical growth layers in a prayer tradition that your chapter treats as politically and theologically loaded.
- It gives a hard transmission argument: not "what should be there," but "what communities actually transmitted at different stages."
- It allows you to separate:
  1) earliest recoverable prayer core,
  2) early liturgical expansion,
  3) later standardization.
